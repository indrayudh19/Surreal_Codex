FIELD REPORT — INITIAL SURVEY

The Resting Place is not so much a location as it is a condition. Knights who have served three or more patrols in the Hollow Sector sometimes find themselves here without intending to arrive. The air tastes of old stone and unfinished thoughts. Time passes, but politely, and at no fixed rate.

The chamber was first documented in 1999 by Dame Elara of the Ninth Watch, who described it as "a room that had been waiting for someone, and was mildly disappointed that it was only me." Subsequent surveys have confirmed that the room adjusts its dimensions to accommodate exactly the number of knights present, plus one empty chair. The purpose of the empty chair remains under investigation.

There are no windows. Light enters from a direction that cannot be determined by compass. The silence here is not the absence of sound but rather the presence of a very deliberate quiet — as though the room itself is listening.
